Netiquette For Email

net·i·quette

The correct or acceptable way of communicating on the Internet or in this case email :)

8 Rules to make your email netiquette better

  • Watch grammar and errors like punctuation and spelling
  • Avoid talking pointlessly in emails
  • Choose a subject with purpose
  • Keep emails organized
  • Reply to emails quickly
  • Watch your tone
  • Use a professional email address
  • Use appropriate salutations

Why Netiquette Matters?

If you want to look good, professional, and impress make sure to follow these rules!

How to Follow These Rules

Watch grammar and errors like punctuation and spelling

Look professional, proof read and consider downloading an effective grammar and spell checker.

Avoid talking pointlessly in emails

It’s not text or social media, emails should serve a purpose. Double check your emails can't be conveyed over a text message.

Choose a subject with purpose

The subject is the first thing the receiver sees, give them a reason to open the email. Make sure the subject is a quick phrase explaining what will be in the email.

Keep emails organized

Delete old emails and avoid signing up for spam mail. Although it can be hard to resist signing up for an email list for 10%off an online purpose, it can help youre inbox from overflowing with junk.

Reply to emails quickly

Don’t keep the others waiting. It is common courtesy to reply punctually.

Watch your tone

Tone can’t be shown through text so watch out for your tone. Sarcasm can especially come off as rude.

Use a professional email address

Don’t be embarrassed to give a significant person your email address. Include your name or an abbrevation of it and maybe a number important to you. Avoid emails that the reciver expects a kids first email to be.

Use appropriate salutations

If it’s a friend don’t be afraid to be more creative, but from, sincerly, or regards is always a good way to go. If emailing for school or work stick to professional closings to your emails.
